# HART (Heat Assessment & Response Tool)

## Practical occupational heat-risk decision-support framework

HART (Heat Assessment & Response Tool) is a practical occupational heat-risk decision-support framework that combines measured workplace thermal conditions with the worker's actual exposure context to support timely field decisions.

HART complements established approaches such as **WBGT** and does **not** replace site procedures, professional judgement, or medical protocols.

> **Good occupational heat management begins with measurement—but succeeds through informed decision-making.**

---

## Overview

HART integrates:

- Wet Bulb Globe Temperature (WBGT)
- Estimated Cooling Capacity of the Environment (ECCE)
- Heat-Strain Profile (HSP)
- Worksite exposure context
- Supervisor guidance
- Audit logging

to provide practical occupational heat-risk decision support.

## Scientific Positioning

HART is designed primarily as an occupational heat-risk decision-support framework aligned with the principles of:

- ACGIH® Heat Stress and Strain TLVs®
- ISO 7243 WBGT-based assessment
- Applicable NIOSH/OSHA heat-stress guidance

HART uses **WBGT** as the principal environmental screening indicator while adding practical interpretation through:

- Exposure context
- Worksite adjustments
- Estimated Cooling Capacity of the Environment (ECCE)
- Heat-Strain Profile (HSP)

The objective is to support better field decision-making while remaining consistent with established occupational heat-stress practice.

## Important

HART is a decision-support framework.

It does **not**:

- replace competent professional judgement
- replace medical assessment
- certify regulatory compliance
- replace calibrated monitoring instruments

Site-specific regulations, HSE procedures, occupational-health judgement, and medical protocols always take precedence.
